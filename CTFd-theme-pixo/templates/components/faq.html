#FAQs

The event is open to all students of the CSE-CTIS and CTMA Departments of Jain University.

Team registration will be handled by the organizers. Participants will not need to register their teams themselves.

Participants are expected to maintain a high standard of sportsmanship and ethical behaviour throughout the event. Any form of cheating, plagiarism, or unethical behaviour will result in disqualification.

Challenges are divided into different categories, and teams can choose which challenges to work on and in which order. Flags for each challenge should be submitted in the specified format to earn points.

Flags must be submitted through the CTF platform using the provided submission form. The format for flag submission will be provided with each challenge description.

Points are awarded for solving challenges, and the team with the highest total points will be ranked first. The event will have a real-time leaderboard to display team rankings based on the points earned.

The organizers' decisions on all matters related to the event are final and binding. Any dispute arising during the event will be resolved by the organizers.

Collaboration between different teams is strictly prohibited. Each team must work independently to solve challenges.
